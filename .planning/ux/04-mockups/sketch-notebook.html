Question
Should we use FastAPI's BackgroundTasks or Celery for our async workload (50 jobs/min, mostly DB queries)?

Stage 1 · Individual responses

Four council models answered in parallel.

For a 50 jobs/min workload with mostly DB queries, FastAPI's BackgroundTasks is borderline — fine if the queries are short and you can afford to lose pending work on restart. Celery is the safer choice once you need persistence, retries, or a separate scaling axis for workers vs. web nodes.

The decision pivots on three questions: (a) do you need durability across restarts, (b) do jobs occasionally exceed the request lifetime, and (c) is the team comfortable running a broker like Redis or RabbitMQ.

Considered three deployment shapes: single-node uvicorn, multi-node behind nginx, and ECS Fargate with autoscaling. BackgroundTasks loses pending jobs on container replacement; this is the dominant failure mode at 50 jobs/min. Celery+Redis adds ~80MB memory baseline but unlocks retries, scheduled tasks, and observability via Flower or Celery Beat. RQ is a lighter alternative if Redis is the only available broker. Final ranking weights durability and operational maturity above raw throughput.

Stage 3 · Final answer

Use Celery with Redis as the broker. At 50 jobs/min with DB-bound work, BackgroundTasks will silently drop pending jobs every time a container restarts, which is the failure mode you'll regret. Celery gives you durability, retries, and a separate scaling axis with very modest operational overhead.

If you want a lighter footprint and won't need scheduled tasks, RQ is a reasonable middle path — but Celery is the answer the council converges on.
